# SmartChef Customer Feedback Plan

This prototype is now meant for customer testing, not investor pitching.

## Who To Test With First
Start with 10-20 people:
- International students.
- Bachelors or people living alone.
- Working professionals.
- People who cook 3+ times per week.
- People who regularly waste ingredients.

## What To Ask Them To Do
Give them the prototype and say:

"Pretend you just downloaded this app. Please use it normally and say out loud what you are thinking."

Ask them to:
1. Finish onboarding.
2. Add ingredients using a starter pack.
3. Try one inventory input method.
4. Look at today's recommendations.
5. Open a recipe.
6. Check the weekly plan.
7. Look at Premium pricing.
8. Leave feedback in the Feedback tab.

## Questions To Ask
- What part felt most useful?
- What part felt confusing?
- Would you use this every week?
- What would make you delete it?
- Would you pay $4.99/month for this?
- Which feature should be improved first?
- What meal problem do you personally struggle with most?

## What To Measure
- Did they understand the app within 30 seconds?
- Did inventory setup feel fast?
- Did they trust the food recommendations?
- Did any recipe make them say "I would cook that"?
- Did the Premium trial feel fair?
- What words did they use to describe the app?

## Decision Rules
- If most users like inventory but not recommendations, improve recipe quality first.
- If most users like recommendations but avoid inventory, simplify inventory further.
- If users say pricing is high, test student pricing or annual discount messaging.
- If users want grocery delivery first, keep it for later unless many people say it.
- If users ask for real photos, make recipe photos the next design upgrade.
